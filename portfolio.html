Volokolamsk Highway Residential Complex
Location: Moscow, Russia Role: Lead Architect

As Lead Architect, I directed the design and technical execution of this large-scale residential development comprising seven buildings across two urban blocks. The complex integrates 102,775m² of residential space with 6,590m² of community facilities, unified by an underground parking structure. My responsibilities spanned from conceptual design to regulatory compliance, with special focus on fire safety and accessibility solutions for high-rise construction.

Architectural Concept Development
  • Designed volumetric composition of 7 buildings (15-42 floors) with distinct facade treatments
  • Developed suspended facade systems using concrete tiles and fiber-reinforced panels
  • Implemented panoramic glazing with safety glass meeting impact-resistance standards
  • Created "shell and core" apartment delivery strategy
Technical Integration & Safety Solutions
  • Engineered fire safety systems for high-rise residential buildings:
    • Smoke-free staircases with pressurized ventilation
    • Fire-rated doors (30-60 minute resistance)
    • Technical floors with 150-180 minute fire resistance
  • Implemented window restrictors and safety glazing above 75m height
  • Designed accessibility features:
    • 1.4m minimum corridor widths
    • Specialized elevator cabins (1100×2100mm)
Engineering Coordination
  • Supervised thermal performance solutions:
    • 150mm mineral wool insulation
    • Double-glazed window units
  • Directed acoustic protection systems:
    • Decoupled floor assemblies
    • Partition soundproofing (≥52 dB rating)
  • Managed vertical transportation systems including emergency service elevators
Regulatory Compliance
  • Ensured adherence to national building codes and safety regulations
  • Coordinated specialized technical requirements for high-rise construction
  • Managed daylight/sunlight exposure calculations
  • Prepared documentation for governmental approvals

VGK-1 High-Rise Urban Complex
Location: Moscow, ul. Bratyev Fonchenko, vl. 19 Role: Lead Architect

As Lead Architect, I directed the comprehensive design and technical execution of this 3-tower complex, integrating residential, commercial, and public functions within strict regulatory frameworks. My role spanned concept development through detailed documentation, with special focus on high-rise safety systems and accessibility compliance.

Architectural Concept & Integration
  • Developed volumetric composition for 3 towers (10-40 floors) with unified architectural identity
  • Designed composite façade systems combining clinker tiles, concrete-effect panels, and metal cassettes
  • Implemented energy-efficient double-glazed units with safety glass (Impact Class 4/Resistance Class 2A)
  • Created "shell and core" delivery strategy for residential units
  • Integrated mixed-use programming: retail, dining, cultural spaces, and offices
Safety Engineering Leadership
  • Engineered smoke-proof stairwells with pressurized ventilation
  • Implemented fire compartmentation: EI 60-rated walls and double airlock vestibules
  • Designed technical floors with 150-180 minute fire resistance
  • Coordinated firefighting elevator systems and heated air curtain installations
  • Ensured compliance with Federal Fire Safety Regulations through specialized solutions
Accessibility & User Experience
  • Developed full accessibility solutions for people with limited mobility (PLM)
  • Designed 1.4m minimum corridor widths and specialized elevator cabins (1100×2100mm)
  • Implemented barrier-free entrances and safety glazing above 75m height
  • Created segregated parking zones with Fire Safety Zones (FSZ)
  • Integrated laminated glass railings (1.2m height) for terraces
Technical Coordination
  • Managed underground parking design (-1 to -3 levels) for 1,605 vehicles with access control
  • Directed MEP integration: Individual Heating Points (IHP), ventilation systems
  • Supervised structural interfaces between towers and underground facilities
  • Coordinated waste management systems with dedicated collection points
  • Implemented acoustic protection: decoupled floor assemblies (≥52 dB rating)
Sustainability & Performance
  • Specified 180mm mineral wool wall insulation and 150mm roof insulation
  • Integrated heat recovery systems and motion-sensor lighting
  • Achieved energy efficiency through advanced glazing systems
  • Implemented BREEAM-aligned sustainable design principles
Regulatory Compliance
  • Ensured adherence to Russian Building Codes SP 54.13330 (Residential) and SP 118.13330 (Public)
  • Managed Special Technical Conditions (STC) approval
  • Directed daylight/sunlight exposure calculations
  • Prepared full approval documentation package
