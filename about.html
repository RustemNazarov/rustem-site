Career Path

1. Formation and Exploration (2006–2014)

Early career: working with facade materials, cottages, and small-scale civic architecture. Gained hands-on technical skills and began initial design work, combined with formal architectural education. My academic background includes two specialized degrees — in architecture and construction — which provided both conceptual design training and a deep understanding of building technologies and execution.

2. Professional Development (2014–2016)

Transition to a more structured architectural practice, with growing responsibility in technical documentation, building renovation, and coordination processes. This phase helped solidify core professional skills — working with regulations, detailed design stages, and cross-disciplinary project standards.

3. International and Team Experience (2016–2022)

Work at the Moscow office of Chapman Taylor, an international architecture firm, on large-scale projects across residential, commercial, and mixed-use sectors. Contributed to design development, team coordination, and BIM processes in projects across the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Central Asia. This period marked a step into collaborative international workflows and strengthened my position as a senior architect.
